SHORT HISTORY OF FASHION :

The history of fashion really begins in the 19th century. The designer Charles-Frédéric Worth, pioneer of Haute Couture, was the first to parade his models on real mannequins in prestigious salons where a wealthy female clientele gathered... undoubtedly the first couture shows in history. In 1900, Paris had about twenty haute couture houses. If the 20th century was the century of enlightenment, it was also the century of fashion that saw the emergence of some of its greatest designers, Lanvin, Chanel or Yves Saint-Laurent to name but a few, exceptional couturiers who only worked for a rare wealthy clientele. In 1930, fashion took its first steps into the advertising market when Coco Chanel and film producer Samuel Goldwyn signed an agreement to dress the stars of the "United Artists" company. During the Second World War, martial restrictions put the Haute Couture houses in jeopardy. The length of the skirts goes up under the knees, lack of fabric, which will mark the advent of the pencil skirt. As a sign of resistance to the occupier, the hat became an icon of the decade. Finished the fluidity peace and love of the seventies, the 1980s slam, shake, innovate. Show-offs and money, the cult of the body, tanning and outrageous make-up: the fashion is too much. Under the leadership of Jean-Paul Gaultier, Thierry Mugler, Jean-Charles de Castelbajac, Rei Kawakubo and others Kenzo, she dresses with XL shoulder pads and fluorescent colors that sting the retina. The woman becomes a "working girl" who plays sports in her lycra leggings. The 80s saw the emergence of the new face of fashion; the supermodels.
